Music is quite nice. So here are some songs I like. Obviously not exhaustive, but generally songs good/interesting enough for me to comment on them or take the trouble to list them.

This page does not contain music I created, though if I do start creating music, it will of course have its dedicated page.

Yoko Takahashi - Cruel Angel's Thesis

Thoughts: GET IN THE DAMN ROBOT

Getting recommended this song was what ultimately persuaded me to try Neon Genesis Evangeleon. Of course, catching wind of angelic and Kabbalistic imagery (all esoteric stuff I'm weak for) helped as well, but this song -- isn't it just magnificient? The linked video is also a montage of many important events in the anime. It's arranged in a way so that it's pretty much impossible to get spoiled watching the video, but watching it now, knowing how everything ends up ... it really just makes the song hit that much harder.

Sixpence None the Richer - Kiss Me

Thoughts

This is a very nostalgic-sounding song I found in, oddly enough, a Lain playlist. I love the recognizable ever-present guitar strumming of this song. A very chill and happy song, great to walk down a sparsely populated afternoon street to.

Super Social Sandals: Sakana Wo Oikakete

Thoughts: Fish, fish, and more fish

The song's title translates directly to "chasing fish", which must have contributed to somebody's decision to put it in the playlist I found it: A Shimeji Simulation Fan OST. To me, at least, the singer has an unconventional vocal style, but that by itself invokes a certain charm, like a child-like, fun, amateur, or nevertheless genuine charm. The general tune of the song is very catchy and quite evocative of the underground internet subculture this song was birthed in, somehow. I particularly enjoy the guitar pattern and the chorus. The band is Super Social Socials (renamed from Anti Social Sandals) and has apparently only existed for 4 years as of the time of writing, according to this article. Generally very cool to see underground music that sounds as good as this.
